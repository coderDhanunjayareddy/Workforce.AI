# Workforce AI Brand Guidelines

## Company

Workforce AI is an Enterprise AI Workforce Platform for hiring, training, deploying, monitoring, and improving AI Employees that work alongside human teams.

## Mission

Enable every organization to build a scalable digital workforce that performs real business operations with enterprise-grade reliability, transparency, and measurable impact.

## Vision

Build the operating system for digital employees, where every AI Employee has identity, responsibilities, business goals, knowledge, performance, and measurable outcomes.

## Brand Personality

| Attribute | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Professional | Clear, composed, and business-first |
| Enterprise | Built for trust, scale, governance, and reliability |
| Trustworthy | Transparent, secure, and outcome-focused |
| Modern | Minimal, precise, and technology-aware |
| Human | Supportive of teams rather than replacing the workplace metaphor |
| Intelligent | Insightful and action-oriented |
| Premium | Polished without being decorative |

## Voice

The Workforce AI voice is calm, direct, and confident. It explains business value without hype and treats AI Employees as operational members of the workforce.

Use terms such as AI Employee, Workforce, Employee Workspace, Knowledge, Campaign, Conversation, Health, Performance, and Training. Avoid bot, chatbot, prompt, assistant, and gaming language.

## Tone

| Context | Tone |
| --- | --- |
| Product UI | Concise, reassuring, action-oriented |
| Investor material | Confident, strategic, measurable |
| Documentation | Clear, structured, implementation-ready |
| Alerts | Specific, calm, and recovery-oriented |
| Marketing | Premium, practical, and outcome-led |

## Visual Language

The identity should feel similar in quality to Stripe, Microsoft, Linear, Notion, and HubSpot: refined, minimal, enterprise-ready, and quietly premium. Use restrained color, clean geometry, crisp alignment, and generous spacing.

Avoid cartoon styles, gaming motifs, neon color treatments, cyberpunk aesthetics, flashy effects, and playful illustration systems.

## Logo Usage

The official logo is Logo Concept #1: a standalone gradient W mark with a teal intelligence signal dot, paired with the Workforce AI wordmark and the tagline "Intelligent Workforce. Real Results."

Use `logo.svg` as the default asset. Use `logo-light.svg` on light surfaces and `logo-dark.svg` on dark surfaces. Use `logo-icon.svg` when space is limited, such as sidebars, mobile app icons, browser icons, loading states, and social profiles.

## Logo Spacing

Maintain clear space equal to at least one quarter of the icon width around the logo. Do not place the logo closer than 16px to a container edge in app surfaces or 24px in marketing and presentation contexts.

## Incorrect Usage

Do not:

- Redraw the W mark.
- Change approved colors.
- Add shadows, bevels, neon glows, textures, or outlines.
- Stretch, rotate, skew, or crop the logo.
- Place the full logo on visually noisy imagery.
- Use the teal dot as a standalone logo.
- Enclose the W mark in a rounded square or badge unless a platform requires a shaped app icon mask.
- Replace the wordmark typography with unrelated fonts.

## Typography

Use Sora for headings, Manrope for interface and body copy, and JetBrains Mono for technical text. Use only the approved weights from the typography guide.

## Icons

Icons should be thin, precise, and functional. In product UI, use Lucide React icons where available. Use icons to clarify actions, statuses, and navigation, not as decoration.

## Photography Style

Use professional workplace, operations, executive, and customer interaction imagery. Photography should be natural, well-lit, and enterprise-grade. Avoid overly staged stock imagery, exaggerated expressions, consumer lifestyle scenes, or futuristic sci-fi visuals.

## Illustration Style

Illustrations should be minimal, structured, and product-adjacent. Prefer diagrams, workflow visuals, dashboard previews, and abstract systems language over character art. Keep gradients restrained and aligned with the approved palette.

## Motion Principles

Motion should be subtle and functional. Use short transitions, dashboard counter changes, progress feedback, and live status indicators. Avoid excessive animation, parallax-heavy scenes, or motion that distracts from business workflows.

## Accessibility

Maintain strong contrast for text and controls. Pair color with labels, icons, or state text. Ensure brand assets remain legible at small sizes and that product usage supports keyboard navigation, semantic structure, and visible focus.

## Dark Mode

Use Dark Background `#0B1220` with white or slate text. The dark logo variant uses a white icon container and white wordmark. AI Teal remains the active intelligence signal but should stay restrained.
